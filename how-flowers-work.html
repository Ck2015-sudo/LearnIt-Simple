How Flowers Work

Category: Science

Flowers are the part of the plant that makes seeds. They attract insects, birds, and other animals that help move pollen from one flower to another. This process is called pollination.

After pollination, flowers can make fruits that hold seeds, which helps the plant reproduce and grow in new places.

Fun Fact: Some flowers only bloom at night to attract special pollinators like moths!
