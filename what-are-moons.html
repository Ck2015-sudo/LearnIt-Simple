What Are Moons?

Category: Science

Moons are objects that orbit planets. Earth has one Moon, but some planets, like Jupiter and Saturn, have dozens of moons. Moons can be rocky, icy, or even volcano-covered.

Moons affect planets in many ways, like causing tides on Earth. Scientists also study moons for clues about the history of the solar system.

Fun Fact: Some moons, like Io around Jupiter, have active volcanoes!

Extra Fun Fact: A Moon Orbiting Another Moon Is Called A Subsatellite, Submoon Or Moonmoon.
