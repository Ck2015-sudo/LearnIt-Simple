What Are Comets?

Category: Science

Comets are icy objects that orbit the Sun. When they get close, the heat makes their ice and dust form a glowing tail that points away from the Sun. This is why comets look like stars with long tails in the sky.

Comets come from far away in space, from regions like the Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud. They are leftovers from the early solar system.

Fun Fact: Halley's Comet is the most famous comet and can be seen from Earth about every 76 years!
