What Are Nebulas?

Category: Science

Nebulas are huge clouds of gas and dust in space. They often look colorful and are places where new stars are born. Some nebula shapes are so amazing that they have names like the “Horsehead Nebula.”

Nebulas can be very big — sometimes hundreds of light-years across. They help scientists understand how stars and planets form.

Fun Fact: Some nebulae glow in colors we cannot see with our eyes!
